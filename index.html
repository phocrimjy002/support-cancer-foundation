Many cancers are difficult to detect in their early stages, which is why awareness and early screening are so important. Cancer happens when cells develop changes (mutations) in their DNA that cause them to grow and divide uncontrollably. Our immune system often detects and destroys abnormal cells before they cause harm. However, when some of these cells escape detection, they can multiply and form malignant tumors. While a healthy lifestyle can reduce the risk of cancer, anyone can be affected. That's why research, treatment, and support from cancer foundations are so important.
